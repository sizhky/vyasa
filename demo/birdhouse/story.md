# The Backyard Birdhouse — a 5-day story

Three friends — **Mia**, **Leo**, and **Aria** — build a birdhouse over one school week.

## Day 1 — Monday — The plan
They decide to build a birdhouse. Three choices: it's **for sparrows**, it will **hang in the apple tree**,
and it **must survive the rain**. One thing they can't agree on: **what color to paint it?** (open question)
Mia will **gather the wood**; Leo will **find the tools**.

## Day 2 — Tuesday — Cutting
Mia's wood and Leo's tools are both **done**. They add a **slanted roof** so the rain runs off
(this backs up the "survive the rain" rule). Aria will **cut the panels**. New question: **how big should the entrance hole be?**

## Day 3 — Wednesday — A change of mind
They switch the plan: **not sparrows — bluebirds!** (this *replaces* the sparrow decision).
That settles the hole question: **a 1.5-inch hole** for bluebirds (the question is answered).
Aria **cuts the panels** — done.

## Day 4 — Thursday — A problem
Leo **assembles the box** — done. But it's **too heavy for the apple-tree branch**.
So they decide to **mount it on the fence post instead** (this *replaces* "hang in the tree").
Mia picks the color at last: **paint it blue** to match the bluebirds (the color question is answered).

## Day 5 — Friday — Finished
Mia **paints the birdhouse blue** — done, the finished house. Aria **mounts it on the fence post** — done.
The project is complete. One new open question hangs in the air: **will the birds actually come?**
